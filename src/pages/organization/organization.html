Organization

Founded in 2012 under the auspices of the International Federation of Gynaecologists and Obstetricians and with the support of major international senior societies, WATOG started as an idea from two young doctors in France – to create a world association for OB/GYN trainees.
Initially proposed in 2008, the simple idea has grown into a global organization spanning 85 countries and representing over 100,000 trainees.
Using a pyramidal structure, WATOG has a 3 yearly elected Executive Board, made up of 2 delegates from 6 main geographical regions. These delegates manage their region’s member countries through National Representatives. These representatives form the General Assembly every 3 years, electing the Executive Board and Officers.
The organization has moved from strength to strength each year, with strong support from senior societies, FIMO, ENTOG and others. Extending across the globe, WATOG aims to continue facilitating the formation and sustainment of trainee associations worldwide.
